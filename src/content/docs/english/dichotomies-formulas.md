Classification of Reinin dichotomies.

Reinin-Model A relation (Rei-mA)  
The following set of diagrams and formulas is an attempt to describe Socions symmetries. Considering Model A a mapping of Information Elements(IMEs) to Functions(Fns), Reinin dichotomies would form special invariants in this mapping. Each Reinin dichotomy can be written as a formula expressing the symmetry in the set of IMEs mapping to symmetry in the set of Fns. Usually it looks like “these elements” are gonna be in “these functions”, and it everything is expressed in informational and functional dichotomies

The classic example of it is Static/Dynamic if you are static then *static elements are present in you mental ring*, as formula it would be a mapping of \[static IME\] → (mental Fns). This is the simplest example of such invariant formulas, they get more and more complex. Actually such formulas and mapping are kinda like building bricks of Model A as a permutation, as a mapping of 8 IMEs to 8 Fns.

The complexity and formula varies from dichotomy to dichotomy. There are several classifications and explanations of Reinin-model A connection. The most well known Western classification is made by Andrew Joyton and described by the following diagram

![][image1]  
Semyon Churiumov’s discovery of Hadamard matrix as socionics structure, postulating strict order on every level of IM and the structure of each level he preceded the Generations of dichotomies theory in socionics, which helps to understand principles of rei-mA connection.

There are 322 560 different possibilities to place TIMs on Hadamard matrix which lead to different TIM-Reinin correspondences, but only the one Churiumov suggested shows very interesting properties:

1. Each level has its own complete meaning and scale of generality  
2. The first 3 levels semantically correspond to Churiumov’s axioms  
3. There is a legacy property to dichotomies which appears on different levels  
4. the legacy and the formula of rei-mA invariant are related to a specific place in system which is all explained by Generations theory  
   

Generations theory connects each dichotomy to the level of Information Metabolism it appears on. Each level there are new dichotomies appearing and the old dichotomies inheriting their fractal (scale-invariant) place.
