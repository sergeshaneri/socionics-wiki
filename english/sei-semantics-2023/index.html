Ego block

Section titled “Ego block”

It’s the most important thing in Subject’s personality.

This is a block of the thinking program.

Ego block is formed by the first two functions:  Leading Function (its ideas contain a result of Ego block) and Communicative Function (a tool to implement Program Function ideas). 

The most important things for SEI are balance and support based on a good attitude and desire to give joy. SEI personifies complementarit: they think a lot about how to help and support people. SEI also embodies consumption and taste. This way they demonstrate their desire to complement themselves.  They consider their preferences and personal attitude towards something to be the basis of their personality. They are confident about demonstrating their desires. They create an emotionally comfortable environment and consumption level for themselves and their close ones.

SEI’s main principle in all endeavors is balance.

Leading function

Section titled “Leading function”

Leading function creates an image of the world.

It contains the major potential of a type.

Each type imagines and understands the world in accordance with the topic of this function.

More often a Subject comes up with ideas on the topic of this function. 

(Topic of each function will be specified in braces)

(Si - Introverted Sensing - balance, harmony, comfort, compatibility, quality) 

SEI is primarily set to comfort and harmony, balance in physical world, so they are often thoughtful and practical. They look at everything in terms of value, beauty and pleasantness. They pay a lot of attention to their general condition, how comfortable they are and what they need now. They think and talk a lot about their desires, as well as about needs and conditions of others. They care for relaxation and resource replenishment. They stick to aesthetic consumption. They often have good taste, at least it’s consistent and conscious. They are practical and they think about quality of household items and take care of them. SEI is programmed on harmony, well-balanced life (efficient living standards) and emotions. SEI may like soft environment, they pay attention to textures of surrounding surfaces, rely on their feelings, style and sense of balance. They pay a lot of attention to products/clothes/interior items match and they love nature.

Creative function

Section titled “Creative function”

A Subject is guided in the world by their creative function. Its topic is what they think and talk about.

This function executes a type’s program.

It’s very flexible and easily triggered. Its topic is usually discussed with delight.

(Fe - Extroverted ethics - emotions, state)

They are set on emotional support. They think a lot about their own and others’ emotions, about how to cheer people up or make a joke. SEI often speaks artistically, jokes and laughs. They constantly play with emotions, intonation, and humor. They like to induce joy communicating with people. If they find a person who needs encouragement, they are happy to support them. Sometimes SEI is stuck in negative emotions and they can tell about it only to the closest people. In communication they use emotions to maintain harmony. They are prone to be emotionally active communicating with others. They aspire to maintain a harmonic vibe. SEI also pays attention to people’s motivation. 

Super-ego block

Section titled “Super-ego block”

Super-ego serves for socialization.

It contains weak functions which a Subject needs to develop. 

SEI considers it their duty to work hard to ensure their future, however, they may be afraid to make a mistake in what they do and it’s hard for them to cope with it. It causes their skepticism and deliberation. It also expressed in a dislike of excessive entrepreneurship, which SEI is able to show either due to an unusual upbringing, or in the most critical situation. SEI considers it unacceptable to postpone life support issues for a long time.

Role function

Section titled “Role function”

This topic causes Subject’s stress. A Subject mobilizes when they have to interact with the topic of Vulnerable function. 

(Ni - Introverted Intuition - time, meaning, perspective, routine) 

A stressful situation for SEI is the one which bounds freedom of expression, especially if it is work-related. They try to wait out difficult situations without interfering. That’s how they use the concept of time as a way to solve problems. When they have any issues in their matters, they may start to romanticize and pay little attention to them or hide them so that others wouldn’t be able to pay attention. Thus they create an illusion that there are no problems. If they do not know how to fix things, they can refer to someone else’s experience. Alternatively they refer to their bad experience and decide that this problem cannot be fixed, unwilling to change their behavior this way.

Vulnerable function

Section titled “Vulnerable function”

The most important topic. If a Subject is not good at this, they won’t feel good at all. Each person has their own education in this function. It’s important if a person’s behavior on this topic corresponds to the idea of what is right or wrong which they and their inner circle have.

(Te - Extroverted Logic - technology, activity, efficiency)

It’s hardly bearable for SEI if their work is criticized. At the least, it causes their protest and negative attitude. Their activity is the most important thing, it’s the heart of the psyche. One should not unnecessarily advise SEI how to do their work more effectively. SEI feels it as an intrusion in the most personal matters. It can be hard for SEI to think through the effectiveness of actions. They may also follow instructions inaccurately because they are too stagnant in their learning of methods. It doesn’t mean that they will always do things poorly, it means that they will do as they were educated. They work as they can and they don’t accept any restrictions in their work process. This is especially relevant to the topic of self-expression.

Super-Id block

Section titled “Super-Id block”

Super-Id is a child-like block according to which a person demands or expects something from the world.

This block estimates if TIM’s environment is acceptable enough.

This is a block of rational egoism, anti-conscience. 

As for people’s qualities, SEI appreciates acuteness and logic. SEI respects creative and dedicated people, especially if at the same time they know how to express their thoughts clearly. They are set to search for self-expression.

Suggestive function

Section titled “Suggestive function”

The most pleasant topic. The subject subconsciously expects information on the topic of Suggestive function. It’s not easy for a person to pay much attention to it by themselves. It feels nice when someone helps with it.

(Ne - Extroverted Intuition - law, potential, idea, possibilities)

SEI is practical and precise and due to that they may not pay enough attention to big picture of any situation, they’d rather cling to small things. They subconsciously expect that someone helps them to understand potential possibilities of a situation and determine its prospects. They adore creative people who have lots of ideas. They get excited (satisfied) when someone explains something to them and helps to understand the point. In difficult situations they can justify themselves by saying that they don’t understand what to do.

Activating function

Section titled “Activating function”

It’s the energy mental system. Information on the topic of Activating function gets an emotional response in the subject’s consciousness. Constant support on the topic powers up a Subject. A Subject gets de-energized if conditions on this topic are unsatisfactory.

(Ti - Introverted logic - structure, causes, space)

They are inspired by accurately and clearly structured information. In many situations the may ask “why?” Their mood improves if they are explained how something they are interested in is designed. If SEI cannot understand a situation, imagine it logically, and does not understand the reasons, it might be a sign of their emotional exhaustion. They are “turned off”. SEI needs deep communication support.

Id Block

Section titled “Id Block”

Id block is an implementing mental block through which the Subject helps others, provides time, work and product of their activity to society.

SEI uses power to support close ones and their relationships. They form a “balanced and correct” (according to their opinion) approach to life in people.

Ignoring function

Section titled “Ignoring function”

The most unconscious topic.

Positive information on this topic puts SEI into a creative mode, inspires them.

This function automatically switches on if SEI needs help on its topic.

(C - Extroverted Sensing (emergency) - load, adaptation, volitional interaction)

SEI subconsciously and reflexively regulates the level of pressure in their life. This is especially evident in interactions and relationships. When it is necessary to establish relations or form priorities, they begin to show their willpower, somehow putting pressure. Moreover, they are not consciously aware of this, like it works by itself. Being concentrated they pay a lot of attention to their willpower and goals.

Demonstrative function

Section titled “Demonstrative function”

The Subject provides their product and work through this function.

They can perform the work by themselves if necessary or give specific methodological advice that will lead to a productive result.

(Fi - Introverted Ethics - relationships, priority, subjective distance)  

If they feel that others need support on the topic of relationships or priorities they try to help. It can be expressed in SEI’s diplomacy when it’s necessary to negotiate with others (preferably close ones). SEI can also behave as a mentor pointing out important daily routine activities.

SEI’s dichotomies and small groups characteristics

Section titled “SEI’s dichotomies and small groups characteristics”

Introvert. Their attention is directed to internal (implicit) objects. They do not like to spread themselves and prefer quality rather than quantity. SEI also expresses it in a great attention to harmony and compatibility, which are the internal characteristics of objects.

Careless. The careless always count on the fact that everything can be different than before. It’s not as if they don’t rely on past experience, they’re just not fixated on it. 

Sensing type. For sensing types practice-based experience is more important than conceptualization. Sensing is the main stream of perception. They pay great attention to details. They are predisposed to particularization and thinking with actual examples.

A Democrat. Democrats do not form an opinion about people (or events) that’s based strictly on their association with any groups. It is important for them to note the difference, to highlight uniqueness. Democrats often use comparative degrees of adjectives and adverbs in their speech.

Negativist. SEI more easily notices missing details and sees a worst case scenario. Even if SEI is an optimist who is used to positive thinking, they will pay attention to negative details to fulfill their social role program. Also it is often expressed in intonation.

Compliant type. A Compliant keeps in view resources that they are ready to invest in a goal when setting it. And if they can’t get success achieving this goal, they can easily decide to spend resources on another one, as if they evade needless spendings. Compliant people are more willing to compromise, and they can easily ask for compromise in return.

Ethical type. Ethical types pay more attention to emotions and relationships. They have a better understanding of this (humane) area. They feel confident showing their emotions.

Subjectivist. For subjectivists emotions is a more interesting and discussed topic than relationships. And structures are more interesting than methods. This means that they estimate relationships by emotional comfort level and if a person fits this criterion, they befriend them quickly. Mentally subjectivism is expressed as a discussion of the world structure or its specific parts. It is important for subjectivists to discuss causes and structures, because when you know how things work, you can always decide how to act. Subjectivists like to come up with new words to denote objects or events. They often have internal (e.g. within a group) terms. “Business before pleasure” usually is not about them.

Emotivist. Some types first pay attention to constructive element in communication, and others notice emotions in the first place. SEI’s feeling is triggered faster than thinking, so first of all they pay attention to emotions while perceiving and sharing information. More often they try to create a positive atmosphere and cheer people up, rather than talk business. But we should understand that it’s just a tendency and not a strict rule.

Process type. SEI likes to analyze events in their evolution process. They know how to organize an “emotional process” (atmosphere). To achieve it they use their aesthetic view of the world.

Declaring type (declatim). Declatim is tend to speak without waiting for an answer, as if they’re broadcasting. They prefer to simplify everything. They speak in such a way that it is very difficult to interrupt them. Sometimes they divide sentences into small ones in order to express themselves more simply, concisely and more steadily. Their manner of speaking can be described as “said with finality”.

Judicious type (reasonable). Decisive types prefer to discuss good timing of action. Reasonable ones discuss the value (harmony) of potential opportunities. The rule “measure twice, cut once” works well for reasonable types. Discussion stage is more important for them than action, because they choose an opportunity during discussion.
It is also important for them to maintain comfort. Reasonable types are easily relaxed.

Strategic type. For strategic types setting goals is easier than coming up with tactical (short-term) solutions. Strategist’s goals and needs are more steady.

Irrational type. Their basic information flow is directed inward and that’s their main feature. Therefore, their way of looking at things is more flexible, they are open-minded and can change the plan after receiving new information. They may go without planning, expecting the situation to change (same about carelessness).

Dynamic type. Dynamic types think the world is continuous (inextricable, extended), unlike static ones. Dynamism is also represented in a sense of consistency. For example, they will more likely tell a story consistently, in a form of sequential events.

Alpha Quadra. This is a group with childish values. The important things for them are to see potential opportunities and long shot prospects, to feel fun and comfort. They are interested in how everything works and can be organized.

Receptive-Adaptive temperament. By a person’s emotion SEI can easily understand their inner condition. They are susceptible to people’s states and moods.

Sincere (soulful) style of communication. They find cooperation successful if participants exchange information about relationships and priorities, form new values, and settle relationships.
